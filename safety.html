Safety Net

Real-Time Alerts for Safer Roads — AI-powered accident prevention using V2V
A project by Sara Saju, Tanya Justin, Anusha Nair & Sneha Jacob

Safety Net is a concept exploration of an AI-powered, in-vehicle safety system that predicts and prevents road accidents in real time using deep learning and vehicle-to-vehicle (V2V) communication. The project focuses on high-risk urban highways such as Bengaluru’s KIA Airport Road, where overspeeding, poor visibility, and dense traffic significantly increase accident probability.

Problem Statement

India experiences one of the highest road fatality rates globally, with urban highways being especially dangerous due to high vehicle density, inconsistent enforcement, and challenging environmental conditions. Bengaluru’s KIA Airport Road is a particularly high-risk corridor, recording frequent accidents caused by overspeeding, sudden braking, and limited visibility. Current road safety systems—including speed cameras, signage, and in-vehicle assistance—are largely reactive, alerting drivers only after a dangerous situation arises. There is a critical need for a predictive, real-time warning system that anticipates risks before accidents occur, enabling proactive decision-making for drivers.

Main Tools

  • AI and deep learning models for accident risk prediction
  • Vehicle-to-Vehicle (V2V) communication protocols
  • Real-time vehicle telemetry (speed, braking, steering, G-forces)
  • Environmental and contextual data (weather, visibility, traffic density)
  • Heads-Up Display (HUD) and voice-based alert systems

Research

Secondary research using Bengaluru Traffic Police (2023) data identified severe crash concentrations along the Airport Road corridor, with Yelahanka Air Force Station (63 fatalities), Devanahalli Airport (58), and Chikkajala (39) emerging as the top three high-risk zones. Motorcyclists dominate fatality statistics at 59%, with 43% of deaths occurring between 6 PM–2 AM. Critical risk factors include overspeeding (37% on expressways), poor helmet compliance (34%), and hit-and-run incidents (30% of fatalities). These patterns reveal the need for predictive, location-specific interventions targeting high-speed segments and vulnerable road users rather than post-incident enforcement.

Solution

Safety Net proposes a predictive safety system in which vehicles continuously analyze driving behavior, environmental conditions, and historical accident data to identify risk-prone zones in advance. When a potential threat is detected, the system alerts the driver through minimal, non-intrusive HUD and audio cues. Simultaneously, nearby vehicles receive warnings via V2V communication, forming a shared early-warning network that enables proactive slowing and safer decision-making before accidents occur.
